Juicy Spaghetti
with Ground Beef Sauce

Slow-simmered ragù · Fresh tomatoes · No onion or carrot

15 min
35–40 min
~1 hr
4 servings

Ingredients

500g Ground beef (10–12% fat)
3 Garlic cloves, crushed
2 tbsp Olive oil
3–4 tbsp Tomato paste
300g Fresh tomatoes, peeled and crushed
300–350ml Chicken stock
1 tsp Dried oregano
1 Bay leaf
½ tsp Salt
¼ tsp Black pepper
50ml Valio lactose-free whipping cream
320g Spaghetti (dry)

Method

  1. Score an X on the bottom of each tomato, blanch 30 seconds in boiling water, then transfer to cold water. Peel, then roughly chop or pulse briefly to crush — keep some texture.

  2. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook 30 seconds until fragrant — don't let it brown.

  3. Turn the heat up, add the ground beef, and break it apart as it browns, about 6–7 minutes. Good browning matters here, since there's no onion or carrot base to build flavour.

  4. Stir in half the tomato paste, cook 1–2 minutes to deepen the flavour.

  5. Add the crushed tomatoes, chicken stock, oregano and bay leaf. Bring to a gentle simmer, partially covered, and cook on low heat for 35–40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until thick and glossy. Taste after 15 minutes and stir in the remaining tomato paste if it needs more depth. Uncover for the final 10 minutes if it's still loose.

  6. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti in well-salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta water before draining.

  7. Remove the bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper, then stir in the cream off the heat for a silky finish.

  8. Toss the drained spaghetti through the sauce, loosening with a splash of reserved pasta water until it clings well.

Tips — With no onion or carrot and a lighter tomato base, the tomato paste and a good hard sear on the beef do the heavy lifting for depth — don't skip either. Leftovers keep refrigerated up to 3 days, or freeze the sauce (without pasta) up to 2 months.
