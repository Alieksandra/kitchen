Banana & Pistachio-Cashew
Granola

No added sugar · Orange olive oil · Oven-dried

15 min
25–30 min
~30 min
~600g

Ingredients

300g Rolled oat flakes
2 Ripe bananas, mashed
70ml Orange-flavoured olive oil
25g Cashew & pistachio nut butter
80g Mixed seeds
½ tsp Fine salt
60g Raisins (added after baking)

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 160°C (fan). Line a large baking tray with parchment paper.

  2. Mash the bananas until smooth. Whisk in the olive oil and nut butter until you have a smooth, pourable mixture. If the nut butter is stiff, warm the bowl briefly over hot water or 15 seconds in the microwave.

  3. Add the oats, seeds and salt to the wet mix. Stir thoroughly until every flake is coated. If it looks dry or patchy, add a splash more oil rather than more nut butter.

  4. Spread the mixture in a thin, even layer on the tray. Press it down lightly if you want larger clusters. Bake for 25–30 minutes, stirring halfway through, until golden and fragrant.

  5. Turn off the oven and open the door for 2–3 minutes to let the hot air escape. Slide the tray back in and leave the door slightly ajar until completely cool. This dries the granola out gently and helps clusters set without trapping steam.

  6. Once fully cooled, stir in the raisins. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 2 weeks.

Tips — Peanut butter is left out here — it tends to dominate over the more delicate pistachio and cashew notes. The orange oil masks most of the olive oil's own flavour, so it reads as bright and citrusy rather than savoury. Raisins go in only after baking and cooling; baked alongside the oats they'd turn hard and bitter instead of staying soft and chewy.
