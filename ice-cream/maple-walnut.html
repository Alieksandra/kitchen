Maple Walnut
Ice Cream

Custard base · Wet walnuts · Cuisinart Solo Scoops

20 min
3 hrs+
~20 min
~325g

Ingredients

Custard

94gValio whipping cream (35%)
94gLactose-free milk
1Egg yolk (large)
7gCaster sugar
63gPure dark maple syrup
¼ tspVanilla powder
1 pinchFine salt

Wet Walnuts

25gWalnut halves
25gPure dark maple syrup

Method

  1. Pour the cream into a bowl and set a small mesh strainer over it. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolk.

  2. Heat the milk and caster sugar in a small saucepan over medium-low heat until just steaming, about 2 minutes. Slowly pour a little of the hot milk into the yolk, whisking constantly, then scrape the yolk mixture back into the saucepan.

  3. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the custard thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon, 2–3 minutes — it scales down fast, so watch closely and don't let it boil. Pour through the strainer into the cream.

  4. Stir in the maple syrup, vanilla powder and salt. Set the bowl over a larger bowl of ice water and stir until cooled to room temperature. Cover and refrigerate at least 3 hours, preferably overnight.

  5. Preheat oven to 135°C. Toast walnuts on a lined baking tray for 12–15 minutes until golden and fragrant — check at 10 minutes, as small batches brown faster. Cool completely, then chop into pieces no larger than ½cm.

  6. Heat the maple syrup for the wet walnuts in a small saucepan until it boils. Stir in the toasted walnuts, return to a boil, and stir for 10 seconds. Remove from heat and set aside to cool completely — they'll be wet and sticky.

  7. Turn the Solo Scoops ON first, then pour the chilled custard through the funnel. Churn ~20 minutes.

  8. About 15 minutes in — once the mixture has started to thicken, roughly 5 minutes before the end of churning — add the wet walnuts through the funnel. Transfer to an airtight container and freeze 3–4 hours. Rest 10 min before serving.

Tips — Adapted from a classic 5-cup, 5-yolk Canadian custard, scaled to roughly a quarter batch — this custard pours at ~280ml, close to the Solo Scoops' 300ml chamber limit but with enough headroom for the mixture to expand as it aerates during churning (per the manual: max 300ml or 2cm from the bowl's top). The wet walnuts are capped at 50g total to stay within Cuisinart's mix-in limit, chopped no larger than ½cm as specified. Sugar is cut from the original and the corn syrup is dropped entirely — the maple carries plenty of sweetness on its own. Use dark or amber maple syrup for the strongest flavour, and keep an eye on the custard as it cooks; at this small volume it thickens faster than the original recipe's 10 minutes suggests. Wet walnuts stay soft even when frozen. Never store ice cream in the freezer bowl.
