Frozen Desserts — Solo Scoops

Coconut
Ice Cream

Toasted coconut · No egg · Cuisinart Solo Scoops

15 min
2 hrs+
~20 min
~270g

Ingredients

40gFull-fat coconut milk
25gGranulated sugar
15gGlucose syrup
175gCoconut whipping cream
¼ tspGuar gum
¼ tspVanilla powder (optional)
1 pinchFine salt
2 tabletsHermesetas (optional)

Toasted Coconut

17gDesiccated coconut

Method

  1. Toast the desiccated coconut in a dry pan over medium heat for 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until golden and fragrant. Tip onto a plate and cool completely.

  2. Whisk the guar gum into the sugar first to prevent clumping, then whisk this into the coconut milk along with the salt, vanilla powder (if using) and crushed Hermesetas until fully dissolved. Warm the glucose syrup briefly if too stiff to pour, then stir it in.

  3. Stir in the coconut whipping cream. Cover and refrigerate at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.

  4. Turn the Solo Scoops ON first, then pour the chilled base through the funnel. Churn ~20 minutes until thickened.

  5. Fold in half the toasted coconut during the last 1–2 minutes of churning. Transfer to an airtight container, pressing cling film onto the surface, and scatter the remaining toasted coconut on top or fold through.

  6. Freeze 2 hours for a firmer scoop. Rest 10–15 min before serving.

For a creamier texture — glucose syrup and guar gum are doing the work here that egg yolk normally would. Glucose syrup softens the texture without diluting sweetness too much; guar gum limits ice crystal growth. Both are optional but noticeably improve scoopability on a low-sugar, no-egg base.

Coconut flavour — toasting the desiccated coconut is the biggest lever for a pronounced flavour. Check the fat % on your coconut whipping cream — under ~30% will give a softer, less rich texture than the dairy version. Adding coconut bits before churning gives a smoother distribution; adding at the end preserves more bite.
